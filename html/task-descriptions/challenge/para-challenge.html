Fortunately, we have made the final delivery of our packages using a drone that was able to hover relatively low to the ground. That may not always be the case! In many areas of the rainforest, the trees can be a multiple stories high - affecting the safety of our drone. If we were to drop a package from that height, it is more than likely that the package will break apart and damage our medicine! To solve this, we will need to equip each package with a parachute

Parachute Notes: The parachute does not need to be “launched” as they are already open when the packages are dropped.


  • Step One: Plan Your Model

    To help plan each step in this challenge task, use your conceptual model. If you need to add new objects and/or behaviors or properties, you can go back to the conceptual model and do so!
  • Step Two: Program the Movement of the Drone

    Accelerate and decelerate the drone and accompanying packages. Remember, the drones can accelerate and decelerate by 2m/s2. You do not need to drop the packages at this time. Simply program the speed up, cruise, slow down to a stop, and wait behaviors.


    Hints:
    • You may want to review your program for the medical truck stopping project!
  • Step Three: Drop the Package

    Now, add conditional logic to your program that tells Rey when to drop the package.
  • Step Three: Make Sure the Package Stops!

    In this challenge, you will need to program the affect the parachute has on the descent! The parachutes are designed so that the y velocity of the package is close to zero by the time the package reaches the ground!
