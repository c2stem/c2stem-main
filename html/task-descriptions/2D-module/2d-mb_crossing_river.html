PLAN and BUILD a simulation that helps Rosa drive her boat directly across to the other side of the river and arrive at the landing strip.


  • Step One: Plan Your Model

    Select the objects you will need to program your boat to move across the river to a specific location as well as each object’s properties and behaviors. Keep in mind what is needed to complete the task based on your experience with the previous instructional tasks.

      Hint:
    • The river velocity does not only affect the movement of the boat. If a rubber ducky were put on the river, what would its speed be?
  • Step Two: Build Your Model

    Program Description

    • Build a simulation for the boat’s motion, so that it crosses the river and ends up at the airstrip in the shortest amount of time.
    • Plot the x- and y- components of the velocity of the boat.
    • Compare the components to the river’s velocity.
  • Step Three: Test Your Model

    It is always a good idea to develop computational models that apply in more than one situation! Rosa thinks that if she uses a boat that can travel at 10 m/sec, she will get there in half the time.

    • Edit your simulation to check if her reasoning is correct.
