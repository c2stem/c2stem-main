In modeling motion, we found that changing the position in a predictive manner is easy, but in order for it to proceed smoothly instead of looking like a bad claymation movie, there must be considerations for time. Because physics has been around for some 500 years, we have computers, and computers are good at monitoring time and repeating tasks. Instructing a computer to repeat a particular task or series of tasks is called a loop by programmers. We as programmers can use a loop to tell the computer to change position during a particular time frame. We as physicists identify this property of motion as velocity, or change in position divided by the time it took that change to happen. You might recognize this from earlier, not-quite-physics science classes as the speed or rate equation (speed = distance / time). Speed and velocity are closely related but not quite the same thing.
