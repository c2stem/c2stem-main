Motion describes how things change over time. Physics explores and quantifies these changes for pretty much everything in the universe--and possibly some things not in the universe. We as physicists begin with the motion of objects, or small isolated bodies or particles we assume have no internal structure. Those changes depend on where the object is, or its position, and how that position changes. Flip books or animations both model motion. How do otherwise non-moving images model motion?
