Activity 1.4.A: Step One - Plan Your Model

In this module, we will not only be programming updates to position and velocity, we will need to program behaviors for Robbie that will allow him to speed up, maintain a speed, or slow down to a stop when necessary.



Hint:

  • - Let’s accomplish this modeling task in parts in order to introduce new programming concepts into your code.
  • - You can build your model step-by-step or all at once. If you forget to add a property or behavior, you can return to your Plan tab to add it!
  • - A good model is one which can be applied to multiple objects in different scenarios. To make a general model, you should model your behaviors with variables, instead of using constant values. For example, instead of specifying the truck acceleration as 4 m/sec per second in modeling the speed up behavior, use the truck’s acceleration value. If you do this, the procedure you write will work for different values of truck acceleration.

Activity 1.4.D: Step Three - Slow Down to a Stop

Your next step will be to slow down starting at a certain distance away from the stop sign. In traffic simulation problems this distance is called the lookahead distance. Let’s call this the “slow down to stop” behavior. To make sure you stop exactly at the stop sign you will have to consider the speed of the truck and the maximum braking acceleration for the truck, which is -4 m/sec per second.



Hints:

  • - See if you can derive the equation for the lookahead distance given the truck’s velocity and braking acceleration. If you need help, check the resource pages.
  • - Again, you will have to use conditional logic to switch the truck from cruise to the slow down to stop behavior.
  • - Don't forget your graphing tool!

How long does it take the truck to move from the starting position to the stop sign?
